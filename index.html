About Meriel

Two decades teaching at universities and hospitals in the UK and Aotearoa.

I'm a UK-trained medical doctor (BMBS, BMedSci) and lifelong educator. Over the past two decades I've taught at the University of Cambridge, the University of Otago and the University of Auckland, and I've worked clinically across hospital medicine and pathology in the UK and New Zealand.

More recently I've been a Senior Professional Practice Fellow at the University of Otago, tutoring medical students across pathology and clinical modules. I've also led the development of digital learning content for academic and industry partners, including long-running collaborations with the University of Auckland and the University of Oxford.

I bring that experience to younger students with patience, structure and warmth. My aim is to help every student feel less anxious about their exams and more confident in the way they think.

How lessons work

A clear plan, gentle pace and exam-board-specific practice.

We start with a short conversation about where the student is, where they want to be, and what's getting in the way. From there, every lesson has a plan, every term has a goal, and parents get a regular update.

01

Free intro call

A 20-minute conversation with parents and student to understand the syllabus, current grades and target grades.

02

Tailored plan

A simple study plan mapped to the exact specification (Cambridge, Edexcel, OCR or AQA), with milestones for each half-term.

03

Weekly tuition

One-to-one lessons in person on the North Shore or Whangaparāoa Peninsula, or online from anywhere — whichever suits best.
